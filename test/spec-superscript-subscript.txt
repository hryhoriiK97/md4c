
# Superscript and Subscript

With the flag `MD_FLAG_SUPERSCRIPT`, MD4C enables extension for recognition
of superscript spans wrapped in single carets (`^`).

```````````````````````````````` example
x^2^
.
<p>x<sup>2</sup></p>
.
--fsuperscript
````````````````````````````````

Superscript is opt-in. Without the flag, carets are emitted as plain text.

```````````````````````````````` example
x^2^
.
<p>x^2^</p>
.
````````````````````````````````

Only single carets are superscript delimiters.

```````````````````````````````` example
x^^2^^
.
<p>x^^2^^</p>
.
--fsuperscript
````````````````````````````````

With the flag `MD_FLAG_SUBSCRIPT`, MD4C enables extension for recognition
of subscript spans wrapped in single tildes (`~`).

```````````````````````````````` example
H~2~O
.
<p>H<sub>2</sub>O</p>
.
--fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

Subscript is opt-in. Without either tilde extension flag, tildes are emitted
as plain text.

```````````````````````````````` example
H~2~O
.
<p>H~2~O</p>
.
````````````````````````````````

When subscript is disabled, single and double tildes keep the existing
strikethrough behavior.

```````````````````````````````` example
~old~ and ~~older~~
.
<p><del>old</del> and <del>older</del></p>
.
--fstrikethrough
````````````````````````````````

When subscript and strikethrough are both enabled, single tildes become
subscript and double tildes remain strikethrough.

```````````````````````````````` example
H~2~O and ~~old~~
.
<p>H<sub>2</sub>O and <del>old</del></p>
.
--fsubscript --fstrikethrough
````````````````````````````````

Subscript can nest inside double-tilde strikethrough without stealing the
outer strikethrough delimiters.

```````````````````````````````` example
~~H~2~O~~
.
<p><del>H<sub>2</sub>O</del></p>
.
--fsubscript --fstrikethrough
````````````````````````````````

Double-tilde strikethrough can also nest inside subscript.

```````````````````````````````` example
~a ~~b~~ c~
.
<p><sub>a <del>b</del> c</sub></p>
.
--fsubscript --fstrikethrough
````````````````````````````````

Double tildes are not subscript delimiters.

```````````````````````````````` example
~~old~~
.
<p>~~old~~</p>
.
--fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

Mismatched tilde lengths do not resolve to either span.

```````````````````````````````` example
~text~~
.
<p>~text~~</p>
.
--fsubscript --fstrikethrough
````````````````````````````````

Long tilde runs are not split into subscript or strikethrough delimiters.

```````````````````````````````` example
a~~~text~~~b
.
<p>a~~~text~~~b</p>
.
--fsubscript --fstrikethrough
````````````````````````````````

Superscript and subscript delimiters cannot open when followed by whitespace,
and cannot close when preceded by whitespace.

```````````````````````````````` example
^ text^ and ~ text~
.
<p>^ text^ and ~ text~</p>
.
--fsuperscript --fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
^text ^ and ~text ~
.
<p>^text ^ and ~text ~</p>
.
--fsuperscript --fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

Subscript can nest inside superscript.

```````````````````````````````` example
x^a~b~^
.
<p>x<sup>a<sub>b</sub></sup></p>
.
--fsuperscript --fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

Superscript and subscript markers inside code spans are literal code text.

```````````````````````````````` example
`H~2~O x^2^`
.
<p><code>H~2~O x^2^</code></p>
.
--fsuperscript --fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

Superscript and subscript spans can appear inside link text.

```````````````````````````````` example
[x^2^ and H~2~O](url)
.
<p><a href="url">x<sup>2</sup> and H<sub>2</sub>O</a></p>
.
--fsuperscript --fsubscript
````````````````````````````````

Paragraph boundaries stop superscript parsing.

```````````````````````````````` example
^a

b^
.
<p>^a</p>
<p>b^</p>
.
--fsuperscript
````````````````````````````````
